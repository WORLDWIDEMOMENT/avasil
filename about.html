Biography

I'm a Belarusian architect, 24, who has spent the last six years working across Europe — Poland, Spain, Portugal, Russia — and beyond. Not chasing credentials, but chasing a question: what makes space feel inevitable?

My professional practice moves from luxury villas on the Costa del Sol to 32,000 m² masterpans in the Dominican Republic, from retail experience strategy at Art. Lebedev Studio to a residential complex now under construction in Minsk. I've been the sole architect, the concept lead, the one who stays late getting the section right.

But the architecture is one language. I also make video, paint on fabric, photograph empty rooms and sell hand-made custom clothing to people I'll never meet. These aren't side projects — they are how I think. The building and the image ask the same question, differently.

I believe the most powerful design is discovered through obsessive observation, not imposed through style. I'm here to blur lines, build worlds that matter — physical and imagined — and find studios where that kind of thinking is not unusual.
